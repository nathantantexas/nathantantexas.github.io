Introduction

Hello! I'm Nate, and I'm passionate about data science, environmental science, and social justice.


I'm particularly interested in the intersection of ML/AI and addressing pressing environmental and public health issues: both globally and in my home city of Dallas. Currently, I'm working on two projects that use physics, chemistry, and data fusion approaches to predict and model air quality exposure. I'm proud to serve on the Jane Goodall Institute's Roots & Shoots Youth Council and the Project Green Schools National Youth Council.

Current Work

Georgia Institute of Technology

Air Quality & Alzheimer's Disease

Fusing CMAQ, RLINE, and ground monitor data through a U-Net neural network to generate 250m-resolution daily pollutant exposure fields for an Alzheimer's epidemiological study in Atlanta. Leading sensitivity analysis on optimal temporal training windows.

In Progress
University of Houston

Urban Air Quality Prediction

Integrating high-resolution U-surf urban surface datasets into the coupled WRF-GEOS-Chem framework. Refining urban canopy parameterization across single-layer, BEP, and BEM schemes to improve fine-scale air quality prediction over dense metro areas.
